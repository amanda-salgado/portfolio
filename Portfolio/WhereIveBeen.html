Where I've Been

I graduated from Weber State University in 2016 with a Bachelor of Arts in Spanish Teaching and ESL. I was able to study abroad in Guatemala and Honduras as part of a Microfinance program for Indigenous Mayan women, and that experience completely shifted my perspective of the world and helped me gain a better understanding of people, cultures, languages, politics, history, gender, and life. I also met one of my mentors, Dr. Alicia Giralt, through that trip, and she helped me to gain new skills and perspectives as well.

Papers & Presentations

I had the opportunity to present research that I did about Indigenous Mayan Women and the different forms of discrimination they experience at the Utah Conference on Undergraduate Research in St. George, UT and the National Conference of Undergraduate Research in Spokane, WA. I owe these opportunties to Dr. Alicia Giralt, who mentored and supported me, and convinced me to apply.
